Who is Bradley Pawlow?

I am currently a student at Northwestern University pursuing a degree in Computer Science and Data Science. Recently, I have developed a keen curiosity for the increasing advancement of machine learning, including supervised and unsupervised learning methods, and its substantial effects on our everyday life. For example, I discovered not too long ago that machine learning was utilized to predict fantasy football points each week for each NFL player. Who would have thought such a popular phenomenon can easily be transformed from artificial intelligence? Trivial findings like these inspire me to discover new ideas and concepts whenever I can, whether related to technology or not, because I firmly believe that knowledge holds the key to vastly improving society and helping others.

Aside from my growing interest in the technology field, I enjoy expanding my interests outside the realm of education. Believe it or not, I am not glued to my laptop 24/7.

If you want to learn more about the coding projects I have designed or have been working on, refer to My Projects, Skills, and Experience page.

Baseball

I am a baseball fanatic. I grew up playing and watching baseball from a young age, partly because my father and grandfather are also huge baseball fans and encouraged me to get into it. However, it took a while for my love of the game to fully sink in. My first ever T-Ball game I decided it would be enjoyable, and I guess somehow appropriate, to distract myself from the game and either pick the grass or lay down in the outfield.

Nevertheless, I did end up maturing enough to appreciate the game and next thing I knew I played baseball throughout high school and still play now in college, in addition to teaching and mentoring younger baseball players.
